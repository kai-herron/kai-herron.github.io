Kai Herron

Graduate Student at Dartmouth College

I am a first year graduate student at Dartmouth College interested in studying low-mass galaxies and uncovering the low-surface brightness universe. I am currently involved with the search for low-surface brightness within Y6 of the Dark Energy Survey (DES) and am working within Burçin Mutlu-Pakdil's research group at Dartmouth. I was previously an intern at Fermilab for 3 terms, and an intern at the University of Chicago working with both Alex Drlica-Wagner on a post-baccalaureate internship.

Interests

Low-Surface Brightness Galaxies

Generally defined as galaxies fainter than the night sky, these systems tend to be heavily dominated dark matter. I am interested in studying how these galaxies fit into our cosmological models.

Dwarf Galaxies

Representing the lowest-mass galaxies that we can detect, dwarf galaxies are sensitive probes for our models of galaxy evolution and cosmology because of their mass.

Machine Learning

As our sky surveys become more advanced and powerful, so must our research methods. Incorporating AI and machine learninig models can help us keep up with the substantial amount of data we are gathering!

Outreach

The only way that we can keep up the momentum of progress in astrophysics is by making it accessible to everyone! I want to find ways to improve how we approach science communication to accomplish this.
